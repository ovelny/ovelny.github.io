Comfort potato

This is a recipe that always cheer me up. It's also a very reliable one, as it costs very little, is nearly impossible to fail, and doesn't require a lot of preparation or concentration. Perfect for bad days.

Ingredients

  • A few potatoes.
  • Some curry madras.
  • Some garlic & herb mix (currently parsley, chive and chervil, but probably works with others).
  • Sesame oil (can be replaced with any oil).
  • Salt.

Since this is a recipe for bad or lazy days, quantities are relative.

Preparation

  • Boil 1L of water into a pot.
  • Wash and brush thoroughly the potatoes. Don't peel them.
  • Cut them in large chunks. For average-sized potatoes, this usually means cutting them vertically in half, then slicing each side 3 or 4 times horizontally.
  • Throw the potatoes into the pot and let them boil.
  • Wait for 12-13 min, roughly.
  • Remove the water.
  • Place the pot back onto the stove.
  • Add sesame oil, curry madras, and the garlic & herb mix.
  • Sprinkle some salt and mix well.

The potatoes will get mashed a little, which is a good thing. Serve immediately and eat.
